Hi, I’m A.K.M. Azarul Islam Welcome to my travel blog, where I take you on an unforgettable journey to the picturesque coastal town of Cox's Bazar in Bangladesh. Known for its pristine sandy beaches and breathtaking views of the Bay of Bengal, Cox's Bazar is a true paradise for beach lovers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Join me as we explore the golden shores, indulge in mouthwatering seafood, and discover the hidden gems of this remarkable destination.

Day 1: Arrival and Embracing the Beach Vibes

Upon arriving in Cox's Bazar, I was immediately captivated by the azure blue waters and the endless stretch of sandy beaches. I settled into my beachfront accommodation, where I could hear the soothing sound of crashing waves from my balcony. Eager to start my adventure, I took a leisurely stroll along Laboni Beach, feeling the warm sand beneath my feet and enjoying the gentle ocean breeze. As the sun began to set, I witnessed a mesmerizing view of vibrant colors painting the sky, casting a magical aura over the coastline.
Picture and Video: Swimming in the Laboni Beach with my son

Day 2: Exploring the Marine World

To immerse myself in Cox's Bazar's rich marine life, I embarked on a boat trip to St. Martin's Island, a small tropical paradise located a few hours away from the mainland. The crystal-clear waters surrounding the island were teeming with colorful coral reefs and diverse marine species. Equipped with snorkeling gear, I dove into the ocean, swimming alongside vibrant fish and marveling at the incredible underwater world. Back on the island, I savored a delectable seafood feast, enjoying freshly caught prawns and grilled fish. In our journey, my beloved wife, Tamanna, sang a beautiful and nostalgic song with a loud voice.

    • Day 3: Unveiling Hidden Gems

    Today, I set out to discover the lesser-known gems of Cox's Bazar. I ventured to Himchori Waterfall, a serene natural wonder tucked away in the nearby hills. The cascading water amidst lush greenery provided a refreshing retreat from the coastal heat. Later, I visited Ramu, a small town renowned for its Buddhist heritage. I explored ancient monasteries, marveling at intricate sculptures and intricate woodwork. The vibrant local markets of Ramu offered a glimpse into the region's cultural diversity, with an array of handicrafts and traditional goods on display. And we decided to take our meal out of Motel for the whole day.

    Day 4: Sunset Magic and Farewell

    On my final day in Cox's Bazar, I couldn't miss the opportunity to witness the enchanting sunset at Inani Beach. As the golden hour approached, I found a quiet spot on the beach and watched in awe as the sun dipped below the horizon, casting a warm glow across the sky. It was a moment of pure tranquility and reflection. With a heavy heart, I bid farewell to Cox's Bazar, taking with me unforgettable memories of its natural beauty, warm hospitality, and the peaceful rhythm of life by the sea.
